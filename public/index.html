    How does it work?

    1
    Do your workout
    (The fun part)
    Go for your training, while wearing your device of choice to collect workout data.
    2
    Import your data
    (The easy part)

    Are you a RunKeeper user? If so good news: you'll be able to easily synchronize your RunKeeper workouts with Fitspector! We will also work on a similar feature for Strava once their API is publicly open.

    If you're not using any of those services you will still be able to manually upload your workout data to Fitspector. It will accept TCX and GPX industry standard file formats. That means you can use most Garmin and Polar watches, as well as GPS-enabled smartphones (both Android and iPhone). We'll make the upload process super easy. We promise.

    3
    Enjoy beautiful visualizations
    (Another fun part!)

    Enjoy Fitspector's beautiful visualizations that will help you reveal trends and better understand your workouts. See the demo below; and that's just the beginning!

    FAQ

    What happens when the funding period is over?
    Two options. If Fitspector reaches it's funding target of $15,000, giving us means to bring it to life then you can sit back, relax and wait for it to come life! (also see the following question). If we do not reach the target we reserve the right to cancel the project, in which case all contributions will be fully refunded.
    When will Fitspector be ready to use?
    The precise launch date is yet to be determined but in the worst case we'll deliver you Fitspector Beta as a Christmas present this year :).
    How will I be able to follow progress of Fitspector?
    First of all subscribe to our newsletter so that we can keep you up to date. You can also follow the Fitspector's blog, as well as follow us on Twitter, Facebook and Google+.
    Who can benefit from Fitspector?
    In principle anyone doing fitness who is interested in gaining insights into one's training. However, most charts will work best for running and cycling activities.
    I'm already using Strava/Endomondo/RunKeeper/...; why would I want another service?
    The above services are great and Fitspector is not trying to compete with them. Instead it will focus on one particular aspect – workout data analysis – and will try to deliver an unique experience in that area.
    Therefore, you'll likely want to use Fitspector along the service that you're using now; not instead. We'll work hard to try to make it as seamless as possible.
    What will I need to use Fitspector?
    Please note that Fitspector is not a data collection service. It will allow easy upload of workout data in TCX and GPX file formats, so for starters you'll need a compatible device.
    Depending on demand we will also try to provide easier ways of synchronizing your workout data such as:
    • Dropbox or Google Drive synchronization, that will allow you just to place your workouts files in appropriate folders and have them processed automatically by Fitspector.
    • Automatic synchronization with the RunKeeper account. RunKeeper seems to be the only workout tracking site that provides API access to user's data.
    Keep an eye on the blog for more details.
    What if I have some other question that is not covered here?
    Just send an email to info@fitspector.com. We'll do our best to answer your question within 48 hours.
